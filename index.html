History of Voting Rights


The struggle for marginalized groups to obtain representation in the electorate of the United States is no new struggle. Since the implementation of the Constitution, only a small portion of the American population has been encouraged to vote. Throughout the nation's history, women, non-whites, non-Christians, and immigrants have fought to obtain voting privileges. Below is a timeline of the history of voting rights in America.
